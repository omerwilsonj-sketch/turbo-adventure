## 1. Philosophy & Guiding Principles

### The "Efficiency-First" Approach

Every minute of training and every meal must earn its place. No junk volume, no wasted sets, no unnecessary complexity.

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Time-Boxed Training** | Every session = 30 minutes maximum. Warm-up to cool-down. |
| **Mechanical Tension First** | Compound, multi-joint movements deliver the most stimulus per minute. |
| **Progressive Overload** | Measurable increases in weight, reps, or density every week. |
| **Metabolic Density** | Minimize rest. Maximize work output in the window. |
| **Data-Driven Adjustments** | Decisions come from logged metrics, not feelings. |
| **Sustainability Over Perfection** | A "good enough" workout done is better than a perfect one skipped. |

### Session Structure (Every Workout)

| Phase | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Dynamic Warm-up** | 3 min | Raise heart rate, activate key muscle groups |
| **Work Block 1** | 11 min | Strength-focused compound lift + accessory superset |
| **Work Block 2** | 11 min | Metabolic finisher / HIIT circuit |
| **Cool-down & Mobility** | 5 min | Stretch, breathe, set intention |

## 2. 12-Week HIIT Curriculum

### Overview

- **Frequency:** 3 sessions per week (Mon / Wed / Fri or Tue / Thu / Sat)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes per session
- **Format:** 3 Phases × 4 weeks each
- **Equipment Needed:** Adjustable dumbbells, resistance bands, a mat, and a pull-up bar (or door anchor)

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
**Goal:** Build movement competency, establish baseline work capacity.

#### Week 1 — Introduction
| Day | Work Block 1 (11 min) | Work Block 2 (11 min) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Goblet Squat 3×10 | 5 Rounds: :30 work / :30 rest — KB Swing, Push-up |
| **Wed** | Dumbbell Bench Press 3×10 | 5 Rounds: :30 work / :30 rest — Walking Lunge, Bent-Over Row |
| **Fri** | Deadlift (DB/KB) 3×10 | 5 Rounds: :30 work / :30 rest — Plank Hold, Mountain Climbers |

#### Week 2 — Density Increase
- Same exercises as Week 1
- Work Block 1: 3×8 with heavier load
- Work Block 2: :35 work / :25 rest

#### Week 3 — Volume Introduction
- Same movement patterns
- Work Block 1: 4×8
- Work Block 2: :40 work / :20 rest, 5 rounds

#### Week 4 — Consolidation & Test
- Work Block 1: 3×8 at heaviest manageable weight
- Work Block 2: :45 work / :15 rest, 5 rounds
- **Benchmark:** Record max push-ups in 60s, max bodyweight squats in 60s

### Phase 2: Building (Weeks 5–8)
**Goal:** Increase strength, power, and metabolic conditioning.

#### Week 5 — Strength Emphasis
| Day | Work Block 1 (11 min) | Work Block 2 (11 min) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Front Squat (DB) 4×6 | 4 Rounds: 45s ON / 15s OFF — Burpee, DB Clean, Box Step-Up |
| **Wed** | Incline DB Press 4×6 | 4 Rounds: 45s ON / 15s OFF — Pull-Up (or Band Row), DB Snatch, Knee Drive |
| **Fri** | RDL (DB) 4×6 | 4 Rounds: 45s ON / 15s OFF — Renegade Row, Plank Jack, Skater Hop |

#### Week 6 — Density Wave
- Same movements, Work Block 1: 4×5 (heavier), Work Block 2: 50s ON / 10s OFF

#### Week 7 — Power Introduction
- Work Block 1: Explosive tempo (eccentric 2s, concentric explosive)
- Work Block 2 includes plyometric options: Box Jumps, Clapping Push-ups

#### Week 8 — Consolidation & Test
- **Benchmark:** Re-test max push-ups/squats — compare to Week 4
- **Strength Check:** Max weight for 5 reps on Front Squat and DB Bench

### Phase 3: Peak (Weeks 9–12)
**Goal:** Maximize conditioning, cut body fat, reveal definition.

#### Week 9 — High Density Conditioning
| Day | Work Block 1 (11 min) | Work Block 2 (11 min) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Thruster (Squat + Press) 3×8 | AMRAP 11: 10 Burpees + 15 KB Swings + 20 Mountain Climbers |
| **Wed** | DB Clean & Press 3×8/side | AMRAP 11: 8 Pull-Ups + 12 DB Front Squats + 16 Box Step-Ups |
| **Fri** | Turkish Get-Up 3×3/side | AMRAP 11: 5 Rounds of "Death by" format (add 2 reps each min) |

#### Week 10 — Power Endurance
- Work Block 1: 4×6 at 75–80% 1RM
- Work Block 2: Tabata format (20s work / 10s rest × 8 rounds per movement)

#### Week 11 — Peak Week
- Reduced volume, maintained intensity
- Work Block 1: 3×5 heavy
- Work Block 2: Short, sharp intervals (40s work / 20s rest, 4 rounds)

#### Week 12 — Final Test & Celebration
- **Final Benchmark:** Full battery of tests (push-ups, squats, 1-mile run or 500m row, max pull-ups)
- **Photo / Measurement Day**
- **Recovery Week protocol:** Deload at 50% intensity

### Weekly Progression Model

| Variable | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Work:Rest Ratio | 1:1 → 2:1 | 3:1 → 4:1 | 4:1 → 5:1 |
| Sets per movement | 3 | 4 | 3–4 |
| Reps per set | 10 | 6–8 | 5–8 |
| Load (% 1RM est.) | 50–60% | 65–75% | 75–85% |
| Heart Rate Zone | Zone 2–3 | Zone 3–4 | Zone 4–5 |

## 3. Comprehensive Nutrition Guidelines

### 3.1 The Echelon Form Macro Framework

**Goal:** Maximum results per meal. No counting crumbs. **Data-driven, but pragmatic.**

#### Step 1: Baseline Calorie Calculation

Use the **Mifflin-St Jeor Equation** (most accurate for busy professionals):

**Men:**
```
BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) – (5 × age) + 5
```

**Women:**
```
BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) – (5 × age) – 161
```

Then multiply by **Activity Factor**:
| Activity Level | Multiplier | Who It Fits |
|---|---|---|
| **Sedentary** (desk job, no exercise) | 1.2 | No training days |
| **Light** (1–2 days/week) | 1.375 | Phase 1 start |
| **Moderate** (3 days/week Echelon Form) | 1.55 | Phase 2+ |
| **Very Active** (daily + steps) | 1.725 | Premium clients |

#### Step 2: Set Macros Based on Goal

**Body Recomposition (most common for our clients):**
| Macro | Per kg Bodyweight | Example (80kg male) |
|---|---|---|
| **Protein** | 2.0–2.2 g/kg | 160–176 g |
| **Fat** | 0.8–1.0 g/kg | 64–80 g |
| **Carbs** | Remaining calories | ~200–250 g |

**Fat Loss Phase:**
- TDEE minus 300–500 kcal
- Protein stays at 2.2–2.4 g/kg (highest priority)
- Carbs take the cut preferentially; fat stays at minimum 0.7 g/kg

**Muscle Building Phase:**
- TDEE plus 200–300 kcal
- Carbs increase first (to fuel performance)
- Protein at 1.8–2.0 g/kg

### 3.2 Meal Timing & Structure for Professionals

#### Time-Saving Meal Template

| Meal | Time | Template | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Breakfast** | Before 9 AM | Protein + Complex Carb | 2 eggs + 1 cup oatmeal + berries |
| **Lunch** | 12–1 PM | Lean Protein + Veg + Carb | 6oz chicken + salad + quinoa |
| **Pre-Workout** | 30 min before | Fast-digesting protein + simple carb | 1 scoop whey + banana |
| **Dinner** | 6–8 PM | Protein + Veg + Fat | 6oz salmon + broccoli + avocado |
| **Optional Snack** | 2–3 PM | Protein + Fat | Greek yogurt + almonds |

**The "Efficiency Plate" Rule:**
> Every meal should contain: **1 palm protein + 2 fists vegetables + 1 cupped hand carbs + 1 thumb fat**.

#### Pre-Workout Nutrition (15–30 min window)
- **Option A:** 1 scoop whey/casein blend + banana
- **Option B:** Rice cakes + peanut butter + honey
- **Caffeine:** 100–200 mg (black coffee or green tea) 30 min before

#### Post-Workout Nutrition (within 60 min)
- **Before:** Prioritize the next meal — no special "anabolic window" panic needed
- **Ideal:** 30–40g protein + 40–60g fast carbs
- **Realistic:** Protein shake + fruit (takes 2 minutes)

### 3.3 Professional-Friendly Meal Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Business Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| **The 10-Minute Prep** | Sunday meal prep: 2 protein sources, 2 carbs, 2 veg — mix & match | Any week |
| **Travel Macro Kit** | Pre-portioned protein powder, collagen packets, nut butter pouches | Business trips |
| **Restaurant Decoder** | Grill > fry, double veg > starch, sauce on side | Client dinners |

For the app / coaching content:

```

🥦 VEGETABLES — Volume & Micronutrients

```

## 4. Weekly Rhythm & Lifestyle Integration

### 🟢 Go Tier: Self-Directed Weekly Template

For Go users running the program on their own — no coach, just a simple schedule to follow:

| Day | Morning (30 min) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | HIIT Session A | Log it in your tracker |
| **Tue** | Active rest (walk / stretch) | Hydrate well |
| **Wed** | HIIT Session B | Log it in your tracker |
| **Thu** | Active rest (walk / stretch) | Check your nutrition |
| **Fri** | HIIT Session C | Log it, note weights used |
| **Sat** | Optional: walk or light activity | Grocery shop |
| **Sun** | Rest | Meal prep, plan next week |

> **Tip:** Set phone reminders for your 3 weekly sessions. Consistency beats intensity.

### 🔵 Core / 🟣 Premium: Professional's Ideal Week

| Day | Morning (30 min) | Work Blocks | Evening |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | HIIT Session A | — | Log macros, hydrate |
| **Tue** | 10-min mobility / walk | — | Prep meals for Wed |
| **Wed** | HIIT Session B | — | Log macros, weigh-in |
| **Thu** | 10-min mobility / walk | — | Prep meals for Fri |
| **Fri** | HIIT Session C | — | Week review, plan next week |
| **Sat** | Optional: 20-min LISS | — | Grocery shop, meal prep |
| **Sun** | Rest / walk | Meal Prep Block (40 min) | Set weekly intentions |

### "Minimum Effective Dose" Week

For travel, illness, or insane work weeks:
> **2 sessions/week** (30 min each), maintain protein intake, prioritize sleep.
> This preserves 80% of results with 33% less time commitment.

## 5. Progress Tracking & KPIs

### What to Track Weekly

| Metric | How | Frequency | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Body Weight | Same time, same scale, weekly average | Daily weigh-in | Trend down/stable |
| Waist Circumference | Tape measure at navel | Weekly | ↓ by 0.5–1 cm/week |

| Energy Score | 1–10 subjective rating | Daily | 7+ average |
| Sleep Score | Hours + quality | Daily | 7+ hours, 8+ quality |
| Macro Adherence | % of daily targets hit | Daily | 80%+ adherence |

### 90-Day Transformation Success Criteria (KPI)

| Tier | Criteria |

| **🥈 On Track** | Hit 2 metrics, improving on the rest |
